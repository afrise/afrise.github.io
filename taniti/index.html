About the island

Photo by Fernando Jorge on Unsplash

Small island, big personality! Weighing in at just under 500 square miles, Taniti is packed to the brim with culture and adventure! From the sandy beaches of Yellow Leaf Bay to the peak of our own active volcano, you'll be sure to find something that caters to your interest. Continue reading to see what Taniti has to offer!

Accommodations

Photo by Zac Gudakov on Unsplash
Taniti has many places for you to stay, from the surprisingly affordable hostel to our own world-class 4 star resort. Want a more personal experience? Reserve a stay in any of our growing number of bed and breakfasts! All lodging on the island is subject to stringent regulations, and we perform regular inspections, so you can sleep soundly knowing that you will be safe from harm.

Entertainment

Photo by Nereid Ndreu on Unsplash
When staying in Merriton Landing, you'll have access to enough activities to keep you busy for your entire trip! We have family friendly venues like our bowling alley, an arcade featuring classic arcade games and pinball machines, movie theater, an art gallery and local history museum, as well as snorkeling, helicopter rides, fishing tours, and a rain forest zip line with incredible views for the more adventurous types. Frequently Asked Questions

Will I need to exchange my currency?
Taniti uses the US Dollar, so Americans or citizens of other countries that also use the USD will have no issue using their money. Many businesses also accept the Euro and Yen, so visitors from Europe and Japan will likely be able to use cash as well. Visitors from other countries can get their money changed at many of the local banks, or use credit cards at most businesses.

Will I need a converter for my electronics?
Taniti uses the same outlets as the United States, at 120 volts. If your electronics use a different connection, you may want to bring a converter, or buy a charger locally at one of our supermarkets

Is it safe?
As with any tourist destination, you should always be aware of your surroundings, as pickpocketing can be an issue, but no more than any other location! Taniti is quite safe overall, and consistently rankes as one of the safest destinations in the world!
